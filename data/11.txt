The essay discusses the causes and effects of unemployment, including factors related to the economy, social, economic, environmental, political, and individual elements. It explains that unemployment is a state of not having a job, resulting in limited job vacancies, limited production, and cyclical unemployment. Other causes of unemployment include inflation, changes in technology, job dissatisfaction, disability, attitude towards potential employers, negative perceptions about jobs and employees, and social exclusion. The essay concludes by highlighting the positive and negative effects, including the availability of dequate labor at reduced market prices.
